Color Refinement

With color refinement, you can refine image search results based on color similarity. By using the color histogram of each image, we can compare and filter results to show images with similar color distributions. This feature allows you to find visually similar images even if they don't contain the exact same content.

Color Histograms

Color histograms are graphical tools used to visualize the distribution of colors within an image. They provide valuable insights into the chromatic composition of an image and are widely employed in image processing for various applications such as segmentation, image search, photo editing, etc. In a color histogram, the values of each color component (red, green, blue) are represented on the x-axis, while the number of pixels corresponding to each value is depicted on the y-axis. This enables visually understanding the distribution of colors in an image.

Color Similarity Calculation

Color similarity between two images can be calculated using various methods, with one common approach being the correlation coefficient between their color histograms. The correlation coefficient measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables. In the context of color histograms, it quantifies how similar the distributions of colors are between two images. The correlation coefficient (\(\rho\)) is calculated using the following formula:

\[ \rho = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N}(x_i - \mu_x)(y_i - \mu_y)}{N \cdot \sigma_x \cdot \sigma_y} \]

Where \(N\) is the number of bins in the histogram, \(x_i\) and \(y_i\) are the values of the \(i\)-th bin in the histograms of the two images, \(\mu_x\) and \(\mu_y\) are the means of the histograms, and \(\sigma_x\) and \(\sigma_y\) are the standard deviations of the histograms.
